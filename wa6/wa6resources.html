What's the Problem?

Getting into waterfowl hunting on Colorado's public lands by yourself can be tough! Limited access, high competition, specialized gear, and a steep learning curve often leave newcomers frustrated. Without community support and shared knowledge, it's easy to waste time in unproductive spots. That's why Colorado Public Waterfowlers exists: to make the sport more accessible, enjoyable, and successful for everyone.

We provide guidance, shared experiences, and a supportive community to help you navigate the challenges.

Here are some of the links that we've found to be the most helpful

On X Hunt

OnX is an essential tool for Colorado waterfowl hunters, helping you navigate the complex mix of public and private lands with confidence. It clearly marks property boundaries, access points, and regulation zones so you always know where you can legally hunt. With satellite imagery, offline maps, and the ability to mark scouting spots, OnX gives you the advantage of local knowledge, making it easier to find birds, avoid trespassing, and maximize your time in the field.

OnX Hunting App

Colorado Parks and Wildlife

Colorado Parks and Wildlife is your primary source for waterfowl hunting information in the state, including season dates, regulations, habitat details, and license purchases. Their website is the primary resource for understanding hunting opportunities, area-specific rules, and conservation efforts that support sustainable waterfowl populations.

Colorado Parks and Wildlife

Ducks Unlimited App

The Ducks Unlimited app provides up-to-date migration reports, weather forecasts, and hunting tools to help you plan your waterfowl hunts. With interactive maps, season information, and conservation resources, it's a convenient way to stay connected to waterfowl activity across Colorado and beyond.
